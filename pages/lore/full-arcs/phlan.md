 ## 26 Alturiak - Valhingen Graveyard
 
 Five strangers met at the entrance to Valhingen Graveyard, answering the call of Doomguide Yorvir Glandon from various handbills posted around the City of Phlan - far to the East of Faerûn in the Forgotten Realms. 
 
 > _"Valhingen Graveyard looks more like a park than a cemetery and stands in stark contrast to the ramshackle, half-built Phlan. Gravel  walkways wend between tall trees, well-tended beds of shrubs and flowers, sturdy mausoleums, and rows of headstones, monuments and commemoratives statues."_
 
 A driving hunger to prove herself as a good person brought one; an opportunity to spread the word of her lord brought the next; a need to rid the world of the Undead brought two more, and one beefy guy was passing through hoping to find the answers to his own questions. Anguish, Elenya, Creswinda, Mardred and Hirsch met with Doomguide Glandon to find that in the past Valhingen Graveyard was overrun by weeds and the undead but was rescued and returned to the glorious resting place they saw before them. However, recent events hinted that undead were once again rising in the catacombs of Valhingen and the Clergy were not in a physical state to defend.
 
 The party agreed to investigate the catacombs, and the Doomguide gifted them all Night vision goggles and assistance in the form of an enthusiastic young acolyte, Cassyt, a female half-elf with more vigour than most Kelemvorites. They accepted her help begrudgingly as she knew the catacombs well and there were no maps for them to take with them, on their journey she told them numerous facts about the catacombs and the various funerary practices in Phlan.

### The Catacombs

The party faced many encounters with zombies, ghouls, and skeletons - pretty much confirming that some form of necromancy was taking place in the graveyard. Our adventurers found it pretty easy to dispatch most enemies within the upper catacombs and resisted the temptation to steal the precious jewels and items left by mourners for their loved ones which gained the trust of their Kelemvor Acolyte. Not all avoided the temptation of treasures in the Crypt - and the group did aid the escape of a group of thieves that had been trapped in the catacombs by a group of ghouls gaining the group the Favour of the Welcomers. Though they didn't end up stealing anything, this tarnished Cassyt's view of the group for a while, though her desperation for friends who were not old men soon overcame her clerical judgement.

After leaving the Sun Pit behind, the group found that the Obelisk set up centuries ago to protect the upper catacombs from any "evil" (modern understanding: undead) had a missing element, meaning that the ward was broken and there was foul play at hand. The group moved quickly through the Tomb of Sedrair II, setting off most of the traps and managing to convince Cassyt that it was better to steal Sedrair's knife and stay alive than it was to be dead without it. They next encountered a neat row of skulls with which the group talked about any strange goings on and deciding that it wasn't wrong to steal the animated skull of a deceased being if they asked to be brought along however, each party member (except Mardred to her knowledge) gained a travelling partner. (See [Items](pages/lore/items.md)for more information regarding the skulls.)

### The Necropolis

The Necropolis is where most new internments are taken and so Cassyt was very familiar with the layout and twists and turns.

> _"It's as if you've entered a subterranean city. The crypts here are modelled after surface homes and other buildings, although they're only about two thirds the size of their real counterparts."_

The party encounters sentry Kobolds and all but annihilated them instantly because Kobolds are very weak, they did however have one Kobold that was quick enough to raise the alarm and so had to quickly run to the Amphitheatre where they found the source of the undead issue - the Red Wizard Rorreth. Along with a Thayan Knight and a crazed Cultist, he led a group of zombies and cultists against the adventurers - but despite this he fell swiftly without time to flee.

Rifling through the belongings and documents he tried to hide and burn - the group found that Rorreth had been trying to raise an army of undead against the City of Phlan for a group called the Cult of the Dragon. He stole into the catacombs and had been living down there for a month raising corpses with the Cultist with the aim to dig under the city to find the Pool of Radiance. Rorreth was clearly trying to pack away anything that implicated the Thayan wizards in the scheme, but Rorreth’s journal linked the Thayan wizards to the Cult of the Dragon and their plan to free Tiamat from her imprisonment.

They discovered that the Pool of Radiance was likely located under Valjevo Keep, and there are extensive maps and calculations left behind from the cultist and Rorreth himself which they took to the Doomguide when they reported that the catacombs were now clear of undead. The Doomguide takes them through a door and tunnel to speak to the Lord Sage of Phlan about the Pool of Radiance resurgent...

## 28 Alturiak - Meeting with the Lord Sage 

Doomguide Glandon led the party through tunnels to the City of Phlan, coming through a secret passageway into the office of the Lord Sage of Phlan. The Lord Sage was old but shows signs of vigour as a Half-Elf and when introduced to the party was pre-occupied by missing scrolls and tomes in the Mantor's Library, though when he finally noticed the group of adventurers - my, did he take a shine to Anguish. The group tell the Lord Sage of the Cult of the Dragon's plans to find the Pool of Radiance, though missed out the parts where the pool may lie within the walls of the city. Creswinda and Hirsch find themselves mistrusting the Lord Sage slightly, though whether this was natural mistrust of everyone, or mistrust of the person himself was not ascertained fully. The Lord Sage offered a gold reward for assistance in helping track down the remaining thief of the missing tomes from the library - as well informing the party that the tomes and the thief himself may help them in tracking down those who are looking for the Pool - he offers the help of the Scribe Master, Cassra Brandywine.

After placating the Lord Sage's advances on Anguish, the party rush to find Cassra who fills in the party on the details that the insomniac Sage has been unable to give them:

-	The scribes started at the library four months before but were caught sneaking into the Lord Sages office 2 days prior which instigated an investigation into them.
-	Two rare tomes had been found in their possession and when they were returned to the archives, incredible forgeries were already on the shelves with sections missing on the Circle of the Scale and the location of their long-forgotten temple
-	Tibeem and Spernik (the scribes) had tried to escape but Tibeem was captured after killing a guardsmen.

Cassra Brandywine was useful in giving this information but seems much more interested in the books that were still missing rather than the missing people - and will offer extra gold for the return of books to the library. Throughout the exchange the party had with Cassra, she only really spoke with Mardred, and it took her a while to realise that there were other people there - probably due to the height difference. Cassra was a halfling and due to spending the majority of her time (voluntarily) surrounded by books rather than people, her social skills had become spotty at best. In the scribes' room the group find robes emblazoned with the livery of a low-ranking member of the Cult of the Dragon.

### Into Phlan 

#### Stojanow Gate

On the advice of both the Lord Sage and Cassra, the Party head to Phlan proper to speak to the Black Fist and the City Guard to see what became of Tibeem after his attempted escape. When they arrived at Stojanow Gate, they soon found that wouldn't be getting many answers from the scribe himself as he was hanging from the gate itself. They spoke to Knight Lieutenant Cron Bolver who divulged that Tibeem didn't give up any of his secrets, but they did spot Spernik heading towards the Dragonspine Mountains - though that’s out of the Guard’s jurisdiction at that point. They received what seemed to be a shopping list that was found on Tibeem's body and told to stop by if they "found" Spernik - as there was a spot for him on the gate next to his friend.

#### Shopping Session
The party had a brief shopping session in Phlan, visiting Cockburn's General Store and buying a Folding Loom (Mardred), general supplies and soaps as gifts for one another. They also checked the shopping list with Tad Staslep - the store owner - who couldn't make head nor tail of the list but had provided everything on there to a shifty looking fellow whose friend was now hanging on the gate. The party assumed he was talking about Spernik, and Tad then offered that Spernik had left his journal at the shop a day or two ago and if it was helpful there were hand-drawn maps in there which might show where he was planning on going.

They also visited Mateo's Marvellous Weaponry where Creswinda bought Cassyt her first set of armour and they ordered some upgraded weapons for the morning after. Mateo's extreme and over the top personality felt off to the party but he seemed otherwise trustworthy and it was put down to being a sales tactic and nothing more - what else could he be hiding?

#### The Laughing Goblin
The Party visited the tavern nearing the end of the day, first speaking to Imizael behind the bar about Tibeem and Spernik and finding out that they kept mostly to themselves but also met with another young scribe names Ellison from time to time. Her main gripe with the three of them seemed to be that they had not yet paid off their tab before they had run off and she wasn't hopeful that this Ellison fellow would be likely to be coming back now he was linked with criminals. She left the adventurers to be looked after by Fat Mar, the tavern server, before going on her way serving other customers.

Fat Mar, or Markoth Hasselpond for those who cared to ask, was a bright portly older man with wine marked nose and cheeks and a warm disposition. He took the adventurers to a table and told them all about the best ways to get to the Dragonspine mountains and the best breakfasts to get in town - he grows fond of the party and asks that they stay at the tavern overnight before going off on their quest in the morning. The group do stay and Cassyt has her first taste of alcohol while a local troupe of bards play on into the night - Anguish begins the tradition of Cassyt wearing booty shorts by cutting her leather armour trousers down to basically crotch level.

## 29 Alturiak - The Trip to Dragonspine
The morning after the night before - the party were worse for wear but collected their ordered gear from the merchants across town and set off following the map in Ellison's journal.

### Crocodile Attack...?
-	This was the first instance of Elenya’s conversion routine, the party were travelling by the River Phlan and noticed they were being watched by crocodiles in the water. Rather than waiting to be attacked outright, Elenya cast light on her shield and began to proselytise to the crocs…and it worked. This began the mass conversion of non-humanoid reptiles in the Moonsea region.
-	They aided in their journey to the foot of the Dragonspine Mountains as the party then surfed on the back of crocodiles. This cuts a two day journey down to one day, they do camp for the night but have relatively few issues. 

## 30 Alturiak - The Temple of the Scale 
-	They reach the foot of the mountain range to find a bunch of kobolds waiting to ambush anyone who approaches them, they deal with this by yelling that they can see them. The Kobolds also get converted to Pelor, Elenya is on a roll. They tell the party that Spernik is inside what is the old Temple of the Scale, that used to be inhabited by the druids of the Circle of the Red Scale. They were also pretty much all called Jeff, this was the beginning of the inside joke of Jeff. I hate this part of the campaign for this reason. They agree to help the party.
-	Inside, the party pretty much used the Kobolds to test the traps and yet the kobolds still trusted them and loved them in the end. With one of the Jeffs inviting them to their colony up in the mountains (they never went) and promising to name one of their children after each of them. 
-	They killed Spernik, but learned the following in the process: 
    * Spernik is only 19 years old, and joined the Cult following his brother Tibeem.
    * They learned that the kid was sent there to retrieve a relic from the temple to try and persuade an ancient red dragon, Scorlworyx, to join the Cult
    * They managed to break into the reliquary and had to fight the Wight of Scalebinder Skovac of the Circle of the Scale, but they won there too. 
-	The party returned to Phlan via Croc-surfing again because why not. 

## 1 Ches - Back in Phlan 
-	The party are summoned back to the Mantor’s Library by Cassra as there have been more thefts and the Knight Aleyd Burral of the Black Fist has made it clear that it will not be made a priority for her. She tells them:

-   Thefts are not uncommon; except they are happening every two days and only from people who reside and work in the library. Nothing of value is being stolen – except valuable books, which points to very particular thieves. The books stolen were:
    * From Aya Glenmir’s office: The Weave: A Collection of Impractical Observations
    * From Marten Foss’s quarters: The Memoirs of Ren o’ the Blade (A long-dead range of repute and one of Phlan’s most famous citizens)
    * From Agin Lamarck’s possession: The Lords and Lineages of Phlan

-	The party go speak to each of the owners, learn more about the books and find that each have a connection to the Pool of Radiance, which is rumoured to be located beneath Valjevo Keep. They end up making a plan to find the pool, they hear that some of the kobolds have been arrested and make a play to visit them in prison distract the guards and instead go down a different corridor.
    * Mardred wildshapes into a panther, she’s never seen a panther and so turns into her best approximation of a panther which it turns out is not very realistic at all. As they bluff their way into a noble’s room to hide from a patrol, this deals some psychic damage upon discovery. The party then gaslight, gatekeep, girlbosses their way through having a talking, moving, medieval painting of a panther with them. 
    * They find some stairs and make their way underneath the Keep and find Ellison – the missing Scribe – covered in blood and surrounded by tomes, including the ones that the party were looking for. They surprised him and killed him and disrupted his ritual and in doing so broke the weave. Magic became very unsettled – every time someone drawing their magic from material plan sources (or those invited to the Aber-Toril sphere as they were drawing from Mystra’s weave) cast a spell, a wild magic effect would occur. 
-	They got the hell out of dodge (I can’t remember how – be impressed with me remembering this much). Leaving the Kobolds behind, this is a source of shame for all of them, I fear.
